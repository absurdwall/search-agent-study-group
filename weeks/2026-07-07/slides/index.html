Agents are changing how we work

“users moved from only asking Codex for one answer at a time into increasingly orchestrating multiple agent tasks over the course of a day”

AI use is shifting from single answers to delegated, longer-horizon work.

Agents are changing how we work

We can see the same pattern in recent Engineering Excellence and Innovations Showcase meetings: many teams are now building agent systems.

Capability is not reliability

The challenge is not merely telling the agent what to do.

The challenge is designing a system in which it can reliably do the right thing.

Agent building is more than writing instructions

“To build reliable agents, start with strong foundations”: pair capable models with well-defined tools and clear, structured instructions; choose orchestration patterns that fit the system, with multi-agent systems only when needed; and apply Guardrails throughout production use.

LEARNING MAY SLOW US DOWN BEFORE IT SPEEDS US UP

This may not save us time next week.

The goal is to build mental models and reusable patterns that compound over the next few years.

Doing the task once may be faster than studying the system.
The payoff comes when the learning transfers to many future tasks.
